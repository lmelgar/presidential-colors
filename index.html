Executive Colors

A visual exploration of the colors of U.S. presidental portraits

When I saw in person the vivid colors of Barack Obama's presidential portrait at the National Portrait Gallery, a question popped in my mind: Has the presidential portrait tradition changed over the years when it comes to its color palette? I set out to explore this in all the presidential portraits.

What you see above are the most prevalent colors in each portrait. This project is just an experiment. If you want to know more about how I did it, you can read about it here.

43 presidents, 16 Colors

Explore how the colors used in presidential portraits have evolved. Or look for what colors portrait your favorite president.

The data used for this project was created using the library PaletteR. This package helps to extract the most prominent colors from an image. You can read more about it here. I decided to extract 16 colors from each portrait. That number is arbitrary. The visualizations were made with Javascript. You can find the code here.

The portrait images came from The White House Historical Association and President Barack Obama's White House website.
